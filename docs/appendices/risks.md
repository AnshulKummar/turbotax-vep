## Open Questions

### OQ-1: What is the right confidence threshold for expert intervention?

The confidence calibration layer (ADR-007) assigns a score to each pre-populated return line. Below what threshold should the system require explicit expert review? Too low, and experts are overwhelmed with flags. Too high, and errors slip through. The answer likely varies by form line complexity and dollar impact. **Proposed resolution:** Calibrate against the 50-return golden test set in MVP; adjust threshold per form line category based on expert feedback during pilot.

### OQ-2: How should goal-fit scoring weight competing goals?

A customer may state goals that conflict in practice (e.g., "maximize my refund" and "minimize my audit risk"). The recommendation engine must decide how to rank a suggestion that increases the refund by $2,000 but raises the audit risk score by 15 points. **Proposed resolution:** Default to customer's stated priority order. Surface the tradeoff explicitly in the recommendation card so the expert can discuss it with the customer. Track which tradeoff resolutions experts and customers prefer over time to inform future default weighting.

### OQ-3: What is the minimum viable competency profile for routing?

The specialty match routing engine (B5) needs enough signal to route effectively, but expert self-assessment at bootstrap may be unreliable. How many verified cases does an expert need before the system can confidently route complex returns to them? **Proposed resolution:** Start with self-assessment plus admin validation. Set a minimum of 20 completed returns with post-filing accuracy data before the competency profile graduates from "provisional" to "confirmed." Use peer review as a bridge for high-complexity cases during the provisional period.

### OQ-4: How does the platform handle expert disagreement with AI recommendations?

When an expert rejects an AI recommendation, is that signal weighted equally to an acceptance? What if the expert is wrong and the AI was right? The B4 learning loop depends on expert edits as ground truth, but expert edits are not always correct. **Proposed resolution:** Every expert rejection is captured with an optional free-text rationale. Rejections are cross-validated against the deterministic rules engine. If the rules engine agrees with the AI and the expert rejected, the case is flagged for quality review (not punitive, but for calibration purposes). Over time, the system builds per-expert accuracy profiles that weight their signal accordingly.

### OQ-5: What is the production PII redaction error budget?

Zero PII leakage is the stated goal, but in practice, what constitutes an acceptable false positive rate (redacting non-PII text) versus false negative rate (missing actual PII)? Aggressive redaction may degrade recommendation quality by removing relevant context. **Proposed resolution:** Establish a PII red-team process with synthetic data variants (partial SSNs, misspelled names, unusual address formats). Set the production target at zero false negatives for structured PII (SSN, EIN, bank accounts) and less than 0.1% false negatives for unstructured PII (names, addresses). Accept higher false positive rates (up to 2%) as a tradeoff for safety.

### OQ-6: Should the customer see the goal dashboard?

The goal dashboard is designed for the expert workbench. Should a customer-facing version exist? Showing the customer their goals and progress could increase trust and engagement, but it also exposes the AI's recommendation logic and could create confusion if the customer disagrees with how their goals are being interpreted. **Proposed resolution:** Phase 1 is expert-only. Phase 2 includes a simplified customer-facing "goal progress" view that shows stated goals and high-level status without exposing the full recommendation ranking or confidence scores. A/B test customer-facing goal visibility against a control group with no visibility.

### OQ-7: How does the learning loop handle tax law changes?

The B4 learning loop trains on historical expert decisions. When tax law changes (e.g., SALT cap expiration, new retirement contribution limits, SCOTUS rulings), historical training data may encode outdated rules. **Proposed resolution:** The deterministic rules engine (B3) is the authoritative source for current tax law and is updated annually before each filing season. The LLM scoring layer is re-calibrated against the updated rules engine at the start of each season. Historical training data is versioned by tax year, and the model evaluation harness includes a "law change regression" test suite that specifically checks for outdated rule application.
